The Prophet Muhammad (S)
Name: Muhammad.
Title: Al-Mustafa (The Chosen One).
Agnomen: Abul-Qasim.
Father: Abdullah bin Abdul-Muttalib.
Mother: Aminah bint Wahab.
Birth: 17th Rabi-ul-Awwal, Year of the Elephant (570 AD) in Makkah.
Death: 28th Safar, 11 AH (632 AD).
Buried: Medina, in his mosque.
The Prophet Muhammad (S) was born in Makkah in the year 570 AD, known as the Year of the Elephant.
His father, Abdullah, died before his birth. His mother, Aminah, died when he was only six years old. He
was then raised by his grandfather, Abdul-Muttalib, and later by his uncle, Abu Talib.
At the age of 25, Muhammad (S) married Khadijah, a wealthy widow. They had several children, but only
their daughter Fatimah (as) survived to adulthood.
At the age of 40, Muhammad (S) received his first revelation from Allah through the angel Gabriel. He
began preaching the message of Islam, which emphasized the oneness of God and the importance of
social justice.
The early years of Islam were marked by persecution from the ruling elite of Makkah. In 622 AD,
Muhammad (S) and his followers migrated to Medina, an event known as the Hijrah. This marked the
beginning of the Islamic calendar.
In Medina, Muhammad (S) established a community based on Islamic principles. He led several battles
against the Meccans and eventually returned to Makkah in 630 AD, where he granted amnesty to his
former enemies.
The Prophet Muhammad (S) died in Medina in 632 AD at the age of 63. He is considered by Muslims to
be the final prophet of God, and his teachings continue to guide millions of people around the world.
